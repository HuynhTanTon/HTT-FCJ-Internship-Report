Here I share my personal opinions about joining **First Cloud Journey – AWS FCJ HCM**, hoping it helps the FCJ team improve the program for future interns.

### Overall Evaluation

**1. Working Environment**  
The learning and practice environment is friendly and open. FCJ members are willing to help when I get stuck. The weekly roadmap is clear, so I always know what to focus on. More social / team-bonding sessions would help everyone connect better.

**2. Support from the Admin Team**  
The admin team explains clearly when I do not understand and encourages me to research first before asking for the answer. Letting me debug by myself first, then giving hints, really helped me build self-learning habits. They also supported onboarding procedures and provided enough documents for a smooth internship.

**3. Relevance of Work to Academic Major**  
The work fits well with Information Technology: from IAM, VPC, and EC2 to serverless, containers, and AI/ML. Many topics were new compared to university courses, so this was a great chance to expand practical AWS Cloud knowledge.

**4. Learning & Skill Development Opportunities**  
Over 12 weeks, I learned how to use the AWS Console / CLI, follow Cloud Journey and AWS Study Group labs, debug common issues, and work with a clear roadmap. The admin team also shared real experiences that helped me plan next steps (for example, aiming for the Solutions Architect certification).

**5. Company Culture & Team Spirit**  
FCJ culture is positive: people respect each other, share knowledge, and help when labs get stuck. Even as an intern, I felt welcomed and part of the Cloud learning community.

**6. Internship Policies / Benefits**  
The program provides a clear lab path, rich materials, and Cloud Credits for hands-on practice. Flexible study time also helped me review and self-study outside sessions.

---

### Additional Questions

- **What was most satisfying during the internship?**  
  Practicing directly on AWS instead of theory only, and being guided to self-learn and solve problems. Every successful debug (Security Group, Route Table, IAM, Lambda…) felt like real progress.

- **What should be improved for future interns?**  
  Introduce architecture review (Well-Architected) earlier so interns understand *why* a design is built that way, not only how to finish the lab.

- **Would you recommend this internship to a friend? Why?**  
  Yes. The roadmap is structured, labs are well documented, admin support is good, and it is suitable for students who want to learn AWS Cloud from basics to modern topics (serverless, containers, AI/ML).

---

### Suggestions & Expectations

- **Suggestions to improve the internship experience:**  
  - Short sharing sessions every 2–3 weeks for interns to present completed labs.  
  - Guidance on AWS certification study paths after the program.

- **Would you like to continue this program in the future?**  
  Yes. I want to stay connected with the FCJ / AWS Study Group community to learn deeper and exchange with peers who share the same Cloud direction.

- **Other comments:**  
  Thank you to First Cloud Journey and the admin team for supporting me through 12 weeks. Even though I still have many weaknesses, I tried my best and self-studied a lot. This internship is an important foundation for my continued growth in Cloud Computing.
